Which park gets the most visitors?

Click the legend to highlight
  • This bump chart shows only the top 10 parks overall
  • The Great Smoky Mountains has maintained the top rank throughout
  • Yosemite has seen the biggest drop, going from rank 3 to rank 8. It also had the biggest drop during COVID-19 park closure in 2020
  • Acadia has seen the biggest jump, going from rank 10 to rank 6

Which season gets the most visitors?

Click a bubble to highlight; double-click to unhighlight
  • Visitor numbers are aggregated across all years
  • As expected, Summer has the most visitors, and Winter the fewest
  • It is interesting that Autumn has more visitors than Spring; this may be due to more public holidays occurring in Autumn
  • The Great Smoky Mountains appears to be the only highly visited park on the East Coast, while the West Coast boasts many

How do visitors like to camp?

Click the legend and use the menus to filter
  • Backcountry camping means tent camping away from designated campsites, with no facilities
  • While Tent camping is typically the most popular, it depends on the park (for example, Hot Springs has a high proportion of RV camping)
  • We can see a similar trend as above, where Summer months usually have the highest overall camping numbers
  • April 2020 had virtually no camping (or visitation) due to COVID-19

When was each park established?

Click the legend to highlight
  • Some lines have been slightly spaced to increase readability (use tooltips for exact years)
  • Line heights do not encode data; they are made algorithmically to increase readability
  • Yellowstone was by far the earliest park established
  • In general, the Pacific West parks were established quite early, whereas the Southeast parks were later
  • The years 1931-1939 saw the establishment of nine parks

Sources: National Park Service, Wikipedia
